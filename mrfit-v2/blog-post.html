5 Deadlift Mistakes That Are Killing Your Gains

By Arjun RaoÂ·Jan 12, 2025Â·5 min read

The deadlift is the king of all exercises. It recruits more muscle than any other single movement, builds raw posterior chain strength, and directly translates to real-world performance. But most people are doing it wrong.

MISTAKE 1: SETTING UP TOO FAR FROM THE BAR

The bar should be over your mid-foot before you even bend down. Most lifters stand too far back, turning the deadlift into a lower back exercise instead of a full-body pull.

MISTAKE 2: JERKING THE BAR OFF THE FLOOR

A deadlift isn't a snatch. Take the slack out of the bar before you pull. Build tension in your lats, squeeze the bar, and push the floor away from you.

"If you can't feel your lats engage before the bar leaves the ground, you're leaving kilos on the platform."

MISTAKE 3: ROUNDING YOUR UPPER BACK

Some upper back rounding is inevitable at maximal loads. But if your thoracic spine collapses on your warm-up sets, you have a positioning problem.

MISTAKE 4: NOT BRACING PROPERLY

Take a deep breath into your belly, brace like you're about to take a punch, and hold it through the entire rep. This alone can add 10-15kg to your pull.

MISTAKE 5: NEGLECTING YOUR GRIP

If your grip fails before your back does, you're leaving gains on the table. Train your grip separately â€” farmer's carries, dead hangs, and plate pinches.

The deadlift rewards patience and precision. Fix these five mistakes and watch your numbers climb without any increase in injury risk.
