The 3 Rules of Recomp

True body recomposition requires navigating a very narrow margin of error. You need to provide your body with enough fuel to synthesize new muscle tissue, while keeping overall calories low enough that it is forced to oxidize body fat for baseline energy needs.

  • The Slight Deficit: A massive 500+ calorie deficit will catabolize muscle. Keep your deficit minimalâ€”around 200 to 300 calories below your maintenance level.
  • Uncompromised Protein Intake: You must keep protein exceptionally high, usually between 1.0g to 1.2g per pound of bodyweight, evenly distributed across 4-5 meals.
  • Progressive Tension Overload: Muscle growth is a localized response to tension. If your lifts aren't getting stronger or more controlled, you aren't growing. Period.
"The barbell never lies. If you are lifting heavier weights at the exact same bodyweight six weeks from now, you have successfully recompositioned your physique."

Nutrient Timing and Carb Cycling

When you are eating at maintenance or a slight deficit, nutrient timing matters. Your body needs carbohydrates the most immediately before and after resistance training. By clustering 70% of your daily carbohydrates around your workout window, you maximize muscle glycogen for performance while keeping insulin low the rest of the day to promote fat oxidation.
